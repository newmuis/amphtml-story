36 Hours in San Diego

Check out these 11 things to do while visiting San Diego

Feb. 16, 2017

Balboa Park

Fri 3 P.M.

Take advantage of San Diego's perma-sunshine with some outdoor time at Balboa Park.

The Crack Shack

Fri 5:30 P.M.

Crack Shack, an open-air restaurant with concrete picnic tables and a bocce court, and a client base that includes what seems like every San Diegan under the age of 35.

The Brew Project

Fri 6:30 P.M.

You should stop by the Brew Project. This small bar in Hillcrest, opened late 2015, is a shortcut to getting to know what's rapidly becoming the country's best craft beer scene.

The Observatory & West Coast Tavern

Fri 8 P.M.

Bands like Beach House and Dilated Peoples have played at the Observatory, a historic North Park theater that's one of the top places in the city for live music and stand-up comedy.

Aqua Adventures

Sat 9 A.M.

Head to the calm waters of Mission Bay (it's easier to learn here than in the open ocean) and rent a paddleboard ($20 for the first hour) from Aqua Adventures.

Lucha Libre

Sat 12 P.M.

It's impossible to pinpoint the best tacos in a town with such a surfeit of options, but Lucha Libre's theme — masked and costumed Mexican wrestlers — lends this place a particular appeal.

30th St

Sat 1 P.M.

Stroll down 30th Street, pausing to explore the district's quirkier offerings, like combination game/bar Coin-Op Game Room, local coffee roaster Dark Horse, and gallery/art supply store Visual.

Liberty Public Market

Sat 4 P.M.

Liberty Public Market was once a naval training center, but it reopened in March 2016 as a food hall jam-packed with stalls and outposts of the city's restaurants, cafes, and bakeries.

Ocean Beach

Sat 6:30 P.M.

Some of the most breathtaking scenery in the area is in Ocean Beach, a coastal neighborhood with a chilled-out vibe and a hippie bent.

Buona Forchetta & Kindred

Sat 9:30 P.M.

Head to South Park, where you can grab a drink at Kindred while you wait for a table at Buona Forchetta, which serves up the city's best pizza.

El Pescador Fish Market

Sun 11 A.M.

La Jolla is a gorgeous small community with beautiful beaches and an upscale ambiance that's perfect for a Sunday stroll. El Pescador Fish Market has been a La Jolla landmark since 1974.
